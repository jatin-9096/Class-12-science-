📚 Book 1: Flamingo (Prose)

1. The Last Lesson

Alphonse Daudet - Importance of mother tongue, patriotism, and the impacts of war on education.

2. Lost Spring

Anees Jung - Stories of stolen childhood, grinding poverty, and traditions trapping children in labor.

3. Deep Water

William Douglas - An autobiographical account of overcoming fear through sheer willpower and determination.

4. The Rattrap

Selma Lagerlöf - Universal theme that essential goodness in a human can be awakened through love and understanding.

5. Indigo

Louis Fischer - Mahatma Gandhi's struggle for the poor sharecroppers of Champaran against British landlordism.

6. Poets and Pancakes

Asokamitran - A look inside Gemini Studios, sub-castes of film production, and political undercurrents.

7. The Interview

Christopher Silvester - Dual perspectives on interviews and an insightful excerpt with Umberto Eco.

8. Going Places

A.R. Barton - Teenage fantasies, hero-worshiping, and the reality-check of lower-middle-class life.

📖 Book 1: Flamingo (Poetry)

Poem 1: My Mother at Sixty-Six

Kamala Das - A moving exploration of human relationships, aging, and the painful fear of loss.

Poem 2: An Elementary School Classroom in a Slum

Stephen Spender - Social injustice, class inequalities, and the bleak reality of slum children.

Poem 3: Keeping Quiet

Pablo Neruda - An emphasis on quiet introspection, universal brotherhood, and halting mindless actions.

Poem 4: A Thing of Beauty

John Keats - Nature's endless treasures providing a perennial source of joy and healing power.

Poem 5: A Roadside Stand

Robert Frost - The economic divide between city dwellers and the pleading rural poor struggling for basic cash flow.

Poem 6: Aunt Jennifer's Tigers

Adrienne Rich - Patriarchal constraints, struggles of a married woman, and art as an escape mechanism.

📘 Book 2: Vistas (Supplementary Reader)

1. The Third Level

Jack Finney - Psychological escape from modern day stress, insecurity, and psychological anxieties.

2. The Tiger King

Kalki - Political satire aimed at the eccentricity, pride, and ultimate helplessness of those in high power.

3. Journey to the End of the Earth

Tishani Doshi - First-hand geological experience in Antarctica, shedding light on severe climate change threats.

4. The Enemy

Pearl S. Buck - Conflict between national loyalty and humanitarian duty during wartime through Dr. Sadao's dilemma.

5. On the Face of It

Susan Hill - Deep psychological effects of physical disability, loneliness, and the power of supportive bonding.

6. Memories of Childhood

Zitkala-Sa & Bama - Dual autobiographical tales exploring marginalization, racism, and social untouchability.
