📝 Latest CBSE Exam Pattern (NEP 2020 Aligned)

CBSE has shifted focus from rote learning to competency-based education. Here is how your question paper will look:

  • Competency-Based Questions (50%): Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs), Case-based questions, Source-based integrated questions, and Assertion-Reasoning.
  • Select Response Type (20%): Standard objective type MCQs.
  • Constructed Response Questions (30%): Short Answer (SA) and Long Answer (LA) questions.

⚠️ The Golden Passing Rule (Must Know)

Unlike Class 10th, the passing criteria in Class 12th is strict, especially for Science students:

  • You must score a minimum of 33% Overall to pass a subject.
  • CRUCIAL RULE: In subjects with practicals (Physics, Chemistry, Bio), you must score 33% in Theory (i.e., 23 out of 70) AND 33% in Practical (i.e., 9 out of 30) SEPARATELY.
  • If you fail in Theory but pass in Practical, you will get a compartment. You cannot combine theory and practical marks to reach 33% if you fail theory individually.
  • For Maths and English, you need 33% combined (Theory 80 + Internal 20 = 100), meaning at least 33 out of 100 overall.
