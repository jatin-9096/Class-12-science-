Shorveer Notes Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 2026

Welcome to Shorveer Notes. We value your trust and are committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your data when you visit our website.

1. Information We Collect

We believe in minimalism and privacy. We only collect data that is essential for improving your learning experience:

  • Voluntary Information: If you choose to enter your name for a personalized greeting, it is stored locally on your device (using LocalStorage). We do not send this to our servers.
  • Usage Data: We track your chapter progress ("Mark as Done" feature) locally on your browser.

2. How We Use Your Information

The information collected is used solely to:

  • Personalize your experience on the website.
  • Help you track your study progress effortlessly.
  • Ensure smooth functionality of our dark mode and interactive features.

3. Cookies and Tracking

We use essential cookies to keep track of your preferences (like theme selection and cookie consent). These do not track your personal identity across the internet.

4. Third-Party Services and Advertisements

To keep Shorveer Notes free for all students, we may use third-party services:

  • Google AdMob / AdSense: We may display advertisements. These third-party vendors use cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits to this or other websites.
  • External Links: Our site contains links to our official YouTube channel (JS EDIZ). Once you leave our site, their respective privacy policies apply.

5. Data Security

Your security is our priority. Since most user preferences are stored locally on your device, there is minimal risk of data breaches from our end. However, please remember that no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. The updated version will be indicated by the "Last Updated" date at the top.
